Reflection
Throughout my time exploring elements of professional development in the student affairs field via the guided readings and assignments, I have been able to commit time to reflecting upon my own ideas around professional development and where I am in my HESA career.
First of all, the experience of taking this Reflective Practice class as an entirely online, asynchronous course dedicated to professional development in the student affairs profession was a tremendous one in developing my skills as a scholar and practicioner. As a HESA scholar-practitioner, there are going to be times in which I will need to take initiatives on a project that made lead to a substantial amount of solo-work. I feel like the structure of this course has allowed me to further develop my skills in time management and given me additional confidence to dive into research and projects with limited supervision. Learning to trust myself to learn what I need to learn and accomplish what I need to accomplish was a valuable outcome of the way this course was structured.
Another outcome of this Reflective Practice course is how much I have come to appreciate and value the personal and professional growth and well-being of HESA staff members. From my practice as a student affairs professional to my exploration of theory and research, I have come to understand in a new way how essential it is for the success of a campus to lay a foundation of support and care for faculty members. Knowing what I know now, I am better equipped to advocate for policies and initiatives that benefit staff members, as well as to take the issue into my own hands and create events and programs through my role to support fellow staff members.
The most prominent challenge that I faced whilst completing this Reflective Practice course was not having access to a structured environment with other students to have discussions over course readings or general professional development topics. In other courses I have really benefited from getting the chance to hear from my fellow students in the Higher Education program and to have a structured way of discussing topics related to the class with one another and the faculty member. Despite this challenge, I was able to utilize this solo-exploration opportunity to develop my time management skills and build the confidence to explore different topics independently, which I think will only serve to help me going forward in my HESA career, especially as I take on larger projects and conference presentations.
In conclusion, I have truly valued this opportunity to dive deeper into and reflect upon issues surrounding professional development in the HESA field. Going forward, I plan on using the information I have gathered to inform my practice in several ways, including serving on the Student Affairs Professional Development Committee, providing supportive programing for HESA professionals at my institution, and advocating for larger level policy changes that not only benefit me as a staff member, but staff members institution wide.
